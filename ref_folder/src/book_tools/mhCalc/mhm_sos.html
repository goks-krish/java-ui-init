Predicted Serum Osmolality  =   mOsm/kg

 

The normal range of serum osmolality is 285-295 mOsm/kg. The measured osmolality should not exceed the predicted by more than 10 mOsm/kg. A difference of more than 10 mOsm/kg is considered an osmolal gap. Causes for a serum osmolal gap include mannitol, ethanol, methanol, ethylene glycol and other compounds in very high concentration, usually small molecules and often toxins. The propylene glycol in lorazepam may cause hyperosmolarity and sometimes hyperosmolar coma in some patients, particularly when the lorazepam is used as a continuous infusion.
